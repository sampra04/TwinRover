How it works

From deployment to digital twin

TwinRover autonomously navigates unknown environments, builds a live map, and detects objects — all without any operator input.

01 — Deploy

Place & initialize

Set the rover at the entry point. It initializes all sensors and immediately begins autonomous frontier exploration of the surrounding environment.

02 — Map

Build the map

Using SLAM Toolbox and LiDAR, the rover constructs a real-time occupancy grid map of the entire space as it navigates.

03 — Analyze

Detect & log

YOLOv8 object detection runs concurrently, identifying and logging items of interest throughout the mapped environment.

Research context

Why this matters now

Scene reconstruction is a rapidly maturing area of research. Techniques such as Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF), 3D Gaussian Splatting, and emerging methods like CUT3R have demonstrated remarkable fidelity in recovering dense 3D structure from sparse image inputs. The demand for these capabilities is being driven by an expanding landscape of spatial computing applications — from augmented and mixed reality experiences like Apple Vision Pro and Meta Quest that overlay virtual content onto reconstructed physical spaces, to game development pipelines where studios capture real-world geometry for photorealistic environments in first-person titles, to robotics simulation where accurate scene models are critical for training and validation.

However, rigorous evaluation of reconstruction models demands consistent, repeatable capture conditions — something difficult to guarantee with handheld or ad hoc collection setups. Variations in capture speed, trajectory, sensor height, and lighting introduce uncontrolled variables that make cross-model comparison unreliable.

TwinRover addresses this gap directly. By providing a standardized, autonomous capture platform, it enables researchers to benchmark reconstruction algorithms against datasets collected under controlled, reproducible conditions. The robot's consistent trajectory, speed, and sensor configuration eliminate capture-side variables — isolating model performance as the sole experimental factor. This positions TwinRover not only as a practical mapping tool, but as a research instrument for the scene reconstruction community, lowering the barrier to rigorous, comparable evaluation of the next generation of spatial AI models.

Use cases

Built for demanding environments

Anywhere accurate spatial awareness is critical and manual inspection is costly, slow, or dangerous.

Warehousing & logistics

Inventory & floor mapping

Generate up-to-date floor maps, track inventory locations, and detect layout changes — without disrupting active operations.

Mining & industrial inspection

Hazardous environment mapping

Map underground tunnels and hazardous industrial facilities where human entry carries significant risk to personnel.

Construction & architecture

As-built documentation

Capture spatial conditions at any stage of construction and compare against design models automatically.

Search & rescue

Disaster site mapping

Rapidly map disaster sites and relay structural information to first responders before any human entry.
